‚ö†Ô∏è Highway Grip Warning

Developer Confirmation (Toplessgun, May 26, 2024): "And the worst grip is on the highways, because there the ice layer is most thin.. but packed.. it's very packed.." The National Route 70 features white ice (not black ice), which is a very thin, packed, and polished ice layer. Despite being paved, this creates the worst driving conditions in the entire game. Expect extremely long braking distances, easy spin-outs on turns, and difficulty maintaining control above 60 km/h.

‚úÖ Winter Driving Survival Tips

  • Highway Strategy: Treat as ice rink. Brake BEFORE turns, not during. Maintain steady throttle through corners.
  • Dirt Road Advantage: Snow/gravel provides better grip. Use dirt roads for safer travel when possible.
  • Speed Management: 80 km/h limit is theoretical. Realistically, 50-60 km/h is safer on icy highway.
  • Emergency Maneuvers: If rear slides, ease off throttle + gentle countersteer. Avoid overcorrection.
  • Tire Choice: Studded winter tires dramatically improve grip, especially on lake ice roads.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Ice Driving Safety Guide

üöó Tire Selection

Studded winter tires provide dramatically better grip on ice. While possible to cross without them, the risk of spinning out increases significantly. Invest in proper tires before attempting lake shortcuts.

üéÆ Smooth Inputs

Avoid sharp turns when transitioning from land to ice. Smooth steering helps prevent immediate loss of control. Gentle, progressive inputs are key to maintaining traction on slippery surfaces.

‚ö° Gradual Acceleration

Sudden throttle application causes wheelspin and loss of direction. Apply power gradually and let the car settle into a straight line before increasing speed. Patience prevents spin-outs.

üõë Extended Braking

Braking distances are MUCH longer on ice (3-4x normal roads). Light, early braking is far safer than last-second stops. Plan stops 50-100 meters in advance.

üîÑ Slide Recovery

If rear starts to slide, ease off throttle (don't brake!) and countersteer gently. Overcorrecting usually makes the slide worse. Small, smooth corrections work best.

üìä Risk Assessment

If your car setup is unstable or you're still learning vehicle handling, stick to regular roads. Lake shortcuts save time but demand skill. Practice on empty lake sections first.

üö® Danger Zones to Avoid

‚ùå High-Risk Areas

  • Marsh Area (East of Lake): Brownish area on map. Vehicles get stuck easily, difficult to recover. No reason to enter this zone.
  • Speed Camera Locations: Multiple cameras on highway. Learn their positions or use ice road to bypass entirely. Fines are 500-1500 MK.
  • Police Checkpoints: Random setup on highway. Check for fuel tax violations (fuel oil in tank = ticket), expired inspection. Ice road avoids these.
  • Techno Victra (Green Car): Speeds on highway, can cause rear-end collisions. If you see it, pull over or drive opposite direction until it passes.
  • Highway Blind Corners: Several sharp turns on Route 70 where oncoming traffic isn't visible. Reduce speed significantly, stay in your lane.
  • Forested White Areas: Inaccessible forest boundaries. Don't attempt off-road driving here - invisible walls and obstacles prevent passage.
