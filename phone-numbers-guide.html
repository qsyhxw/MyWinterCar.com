3. How to Dial Correctly (Step-by-Step)

The phone system is unforgiving. Follow these exact steps or your call will fail:

1 Pick Up the Receiver

Approach the phone and press E (or your interact key) to pick up. You should hear a dial tone.

2 Use ONLY Keyboard Numpad

Critical: You MUST use the numpad on the right side of your keyboard. Top row numbers (1-9 above letters) DO NOT WORK.

‚ö†Ô∏è No Numpad? Try This:
  • Laptop users: Many laptops have hidden numpad - press Fn + Num Lock, then use J/K/L/U/I/O keys (look for small numbers printed on keys)
  • Windows On-Screen Keyboard: Press Win + Ctrl + O, click "Options", enable numpad
  • Last resort: External USB numpad (~$10-15)
3 Type Number Quickly (No Dashes)

Format: Ignore dashes in written numbers. Example: 08-712112 ‚Üí type 08712112

Speed: Type continuously without pausing >1 second between digits, or the game auto-dials an incomplete number.

4 Wait for Connection

After typing the full number, you'll hear beeping/ringing. Be patient - NPCs take 5-30 seconds to pick up depending on:

  • Time of day - Some NPCs have business hours
  • Game state - First-time calls may take longer
  • NPC availability - They won't answer at 3 AM
5 Listen to ENTIRE Conversation

DO NOT HANG UP EARLY! You MUST wait until:

  1. The NPC finishes speaking (you'll hear their full dialogue)
  2. They hang up first (line goes dead / dial tone returns)
  3. Only then put down the receiver

Why this matters: Hanging up early prevents the game event from triggering - no map marker, no job activation, no parts order. You'll have to call again.

‚ö†Ô∏è Troubleshooting: "Phone Rings Endlessly"

If you hear continuous ringing with no answer:

  • Try different time of day - Call between 09:00-15:00 for best results
  • Verify number - Double-check you typed correctly (8 digits, no dashes)

5. Parts Ordering System

Ordering parts requires a different phone workflow. Each part has a unique phone number printed in magazines. For comprehensive sourcing strategies, see our parts acquisition guide.

Two Parts Catalogs:

üì∞
Classifieds Magazine

Cost: 15 mk at PSK gas station shop

Content: Used parts from individual sellers (engine, body, suspension)

Updates: Weekly reset - new listings every Monday

Numbers: Change each week - must buy new magazine

üìò
AMIS Catalog

Cost: Free (found in parents' garage)

Content: New performance/tuning parts

Updates: Static catalog with consistent part numbers

Quality: Brand new parts vs. used condition in Classifieds

Parts Ordering Process:

1 Buy/Find the Magazine

Purchase Classifieds Magazine (15 mk) at PSK shop, or grab AMIS catalog from parents' garage workbench.

2 Go to Any Phone

Use phone at apartment or parents' house. Pick up receiver to get dial tone.

3 Open Magazine While Holding Phone

Press F key to open the magazine. You'll see pages with parts listings and phone numbers.

4 Type the Part's Phone Number

Using numpad, type the 8-digit number printed under the part you want. Example: 08824192

5 Wait for Call to Complete

Listen to the seller's speech. Do not hang up until they disconnect first. The magazine listing number will change to 0 if order succeeded.

6 Check Mailbox in 2-3 Days

Delivery takes 40-200 in-game minutes (~2-3 days). You'll receive a notice in your apartment mailbox when parts arrive at PSK.

7 Pick Up at PSK After 10:00 AM

Drive to PSK, go to counter, pay for your order. The sealed box spawns on the left side of counter. Carry parts to your vehicle.

‚ö†Ô∏è Parts Ordering Mistakes to Avoid
  • Hanging up too early - Order won't register, you lose the attempt
  • Ordering without budget - Calling commits you to purchase immediately (no refunds)
  • Not checking mailbox - Parts won't appear at PSK until you see the delivery notice
  • Using old magazine - Classifieds reset weekly, old numbers won't work
