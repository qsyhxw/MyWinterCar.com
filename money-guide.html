1. Money System Overview

Money (markka, mk) is essential in My Winter Car. You need it for:

  • Food & Survival: ~50-100mk daily at Teimo's shop
  • Rivett Purchase: ~500mk for the car body
  • Parts Acquisition: 200+ parts ranging from 50mk-500mk+ each
  • Fuel: Regular refueling costs
  • Vehicle Maintenance: Repairs, inspection fees
  • Total Rivett Build Cost: Estimated 15,000-30,000mk+ (unconfirmed)
üí° Banking System

Unlike My Summer Car's cash-only system, MWC features bank accounts:

  • Automatic deposits: Job salaries go directly to bank
  • ATM access: Check balance and withdraw cash
  • Automatic tax deduction: Salaries are after-tax amounts
  • Cash still needed: For shops, gas stations, most purchases

Starting Money

You begin with approximately 1,000-1,500mk in cash and bank combined. This is barely enough for 2-3 weeks of survival without income. Getting a job immediately is critical.

3. Futufon Office Job (Most Reliable)

üì± Futufon Mobile Phone Factory
2,470 - 2,910 mk/week

Developer Confirmed Salary: This is the ONLY job with verified EA salary data.

Trial Period (Weeks 1-2)
2,470 mk/week
Permanent (Week 3+)
2,910 mk/week
Monthly Income
~11,640 mk
Hourly Rate (approx)
~73 mk/hour

How to Apply

1Drive to Futufon Office

Location: Near Loppe exit on highway, beside bridge. Same building as MSC's Kotimuoti factory.

  • From apartment: Drive towards Loppe, look for highway exit
  • Large industrial building on left side
  • Park in employee lot
  • Vehicle needed: Use your Sorbett for daily commute
2Enter and Find Reception
  • Walk through main entrance
  • Reception desk immediately on your right
  • Receptionist: Suski
3Complete Interview Process

Step-by-step dialogue:

  1. Talk to Suski - she'll direct you to manager
  2. Find manager Mr. Teffelgren's office upstairs
  3. IMPORTANT: Keep pressing K to continue dialogue
  4. Manager will ask about your availability
  5. After interview, return to reception
  6. You're hired! Start Monday 7:00 AM

Daily Work Routine

Work Hours: Monday-Friday, 7:00-17:00 (10 hours, includes breaks)

Job Task: Assemble Futufon mobile phone charger packages

Assembly Process:

  1. Go to your workstation (assigned desk)
  2. Pick up charging cable from supply pile
  3. Pick up manual/instructions from supply pile
  4. Place both items into plastic tray
  5. Place filled tray into cardboard box
  6. Put completed box on outgoing pallet
  7. Repeat for 8+ hours
‚ö†Ô∏è Job Performance Matters

Boss is watching! Developer confirmed:

  • Manager monitors work efficiency
  • Slacking off too much = fired
  • Missing too many days = fired
  • Arriving late repeatedly = warning/fired
  • You CAN be dismissed and must reapply

Payment System

  • Frequency: Every week (likely Friday)
  • Method: Direct deposit to bank account
  • Taxes: Already deducted (amounts shown are take-home)
  • Access: Withdraw cash from ATM anytime

Overtime & Breaks

Standard shift: 8 hours required minimum
Overtime available: Can work 2-3 extra hours for additional pay (rate unconfirmed)
Breaks: Built into 10-hour schedule

‚úÖ Advantages

  • Highest VERIFIED steady income
  • Guaranteed weekly pay
  • No vehicle wear (just commute)
  • Simple, repetitive work
  • Build routine for survival management
  • Salary increases after trial period

‚ùå Disadvantages

  • Time-intensive (40 hrs/week)
  • Limited Rivett building time
  • Boring, repetitive gameplay
  • Can be fired for poor performance
  • Must commute daily (fuel cost)
  • Fatigue management critical
üí° Futufon Strategy Tips
  • Best for beginners: Guaranteed income while learning game
  • Work 2-4 weeks: Save 10,000-15,000mk, then quit to build Rivett
  • Sleep management: Go to bed by 23:00 to wake naturally for 7:00 shift
  • Meal prep: Buy 5 days food Sunday, keep in office fridge (if available)
  • Weekend building: Use Sat-Sun for Rivett work

4. Firewood Delivery (Potentially Highest Single Income)

ü™µ Firewood Chopping & Delivery
~3,200 mk? (Unconfirmed)
‚ö†Ô∏è Data Status: UNCONFIRMED

Official Wiki Statement: "It is yet unknown how much money can be earned from this job, but it might stay the same amount of 3,200 mk like in My Summer Car."

This is speculation based on MSC. No EA tester has confirmed the actual payout.

Firewood delivery involves chopping logs at your parents' house, loading them onto Kekmet tractor's trailer, and delivering to the sale destination.

Requirements

  • Vehicle: Kekmet tractor + trailer (found at parents' house)
  • Tool: Axe (at parents' house woodshed)
  • Location: Must use parents' house property
  • Time investment: Estimated 5-8 hours real-time for full load

Process Overview

1Chop Firewood
  • Find log piles at parents' property
  • Equip axe, chop logs into firewood pieces
  • Stack chopped wood in accessible location
  • Tedious process: Must chop many individual logs
2Load Trailer
  • Hitch trailer to Kekmet tractor
  • Manually load firewood pieces onto trailer
  • Stack efficiently to maximize load
  • Physical work: Carry each piece individually
3Drive to Delivery Location
  • Delivery address given when job accepted (mechanism unclear in EA)
  • Drive Kekmet carefully - trailer can tip
  • Tractor is slow (~30-40 km/h max)
  • Watch for icy roads
4Unload & Collect Payment
  • Unload firewood at customer location
  • Payment method unclear (cash? bank transfer?)
  • Return home and repeat

‚úÖ Advantages (If 3,200mk confirmed)

  • Potentially highest single-job payout
  • Flexible timing - work when you want
  • Repeatable income source
  • Reduces stress (chopping wood lowers stress stat)
  • No boss to answer to
  • Can quit anytime

‚ùå Disadvantages

  • UNCONFIRMED PAYOUT - might pay less than 3,200mk
  • Extremely time-consuming (5-8 hours)
  • Physically tedious - repetitive chopping
  • Requires Kekmet (slow vehicle)
  • Fatigue-inducing activity
  • Weather-dependent (harder in blizzards)
üí° Strategy: Worth It?

Current Recommendation: WAIT for community confirmation of payout before investing 5-8 hours.

If confirmed at 3,200mk: Excellent supplementary income - do 1-2 deliveries per month alongside other work.

If less than 2,000mk: Not worth the time investment compared to Futufon.

5. Advertisement Delivery (Most Flexible)

üì∞ Advertisement/Flyer Delivery
Variable (Per delivery)

Confirmed Details: Job exists, pays via bank transfer (Jakopalkkio) every Friday. Amount based on delivery quantity.

How to Start

1Find Phone Number

Location: PSK gas station information board (inside shop, right side as you enter)

Look for advertisement delivery listing, note the phone number.

2Call to Sign Up
  • Use phone at apartment or parents' house
  • Dial the number (use numpad)
  • Listen to entire conversation
  • You're registered!
3Receive Advertisements

Delivery schedule: Advertisements appear at your doorstep (apartment or parents' house) after signing up.

Player report: "Friday morning a new set of Advert sheets were at my doorstep."

Likely weekly batches delivered.

4Distribute Flyers
  • Load advertisements into your vehicle
  • Drive to mailboxes around map
  • Place one advertisement in each mailbox
  • Cover as many houses as possible

Tips:

  • Use Sorbett or Gifu for capacity
  • Plan efficient route (minimize backtracking)
  • Mark delivered locations mentally
5Get Paid

Payment: Every Friday, money automatically deposited to bank account via Jakopalkkio system.

Amount: Based on number of deliveries made that week.

Unconfirmed: Per-delivery rate unknown (might be 5-20mk per mailbox?)

‚úÖ Advantages

  • Work at your own pace
  • Can combine with other activities
  • Low stress, easy work
  • Use any vehicle
  • Automatic weekly payment
  • No boss, no performance pressure

‚ùå Disadvantages

  • Unknown pay rate - might be very low
  • Time spent driving vs. income unclear
  • Fuel costs eat into profit
  • Repetitive driving
  • Weather makes deliveries harder
  • Many mailboxes spread across large map
üí° Advertisement Strategy

Best as supplementary income: Do deliveries while running other errands.

üèÅ Competitive Racing
Prize Money (Unknown)

Two racing events alternate bi-weekly: Rally Sprint Race (every other weekend) and Ice Track Race (opposite weekends, Fri-Sun). Full details in our Racing Guide.

Rally Sprint Race

Track: Point-to-point rally stage through forest roads
Schedule: Every other weekend
Vehicle: Your built Rivett (must be functional)

‚ö†Ô∏è Prize Money Unknown

Wiki states: "Assumed similar to My Summer Car" but EA prize amounts not confirmed.

Requirements:

  • Fully assembled, running Rivett
  • Strong engine build recommended (tuned for power)
  • Good suspension setup
  • Driver skill - rally driving is difficult

Ice Track Race

Track: Frozen lake circuit
Schedule: Friday-Sunday, alternates with Rally
Surface: Ice - requires studded winter tires

Do not attempt until you're skilled and car is fully tuned.

7. Other Income Sources

Flea Market Sales

Confirmed mechanic: Rent a table at the flea market (same building as inspection), sell your items.

How it works:

  • Bring items to flea market
  • Rent a display table
  • Set prices for your items
  • Leave items on table
  • Weekly settlement: "You will get all the money when the week pass"
  • Unsold items remain on your table

What to sell:

  • Extra Rivett parts you don't need
  • Duplicate parts from Parts Magazine
  • Old/worn parts after upgrades
  • Found items around map

Income potential: Variable, depends entirely on what you sell and pricing

üí° Flea Market Strategy
  • Not primary income - supplementary only
  • Good for clearing out junk
  • Price competitively (check what others charge)
  • Don't expect big money unless selling valuable parts

8. Optimal Money Making Strategies

Early Game (Days 1-14): Survival Phase

üéØ Strategy: Get Futufon Job ASAP

Why Futufon first:

  • ONLY job with confirmed, reliable income
  • Guaranteed 2,470-2,910mk per week
  • Eliminates survival stress
  • Build financial buffer

Week 1-2 Plan:

  1. Day 1: Apply to Futufon, get hired
  2. Day 2 (Monday): Start work, earn first paycheck
  3. Week 1 income: 2,470mk
  4. Week 2 income: 2,470mk
  5. Total after 2 weeks: ~4,940mk + starting money = 6,000-6,500mk

Expenses during this period:

  • Food: ~700-1,000mk (2 weeks)
  • Fuel: ~300-500mk (commuting)
  • Net savings: ~4,500-5,500mk

Mid Game (Weeks 3-8): Accumulation Phase

üí∞ Strategy: Save for Rivett Build

Goal: Accumulate 15,000-20,000mk for Rivett purchase + initial parts

Option A: Stay at Futufon

  • Week 3+: Salary increases to 2,910mk/week
  • 4 more weeks: 2,910 √ó 4 = 11,640mk
  • Total after 6 weeks: ~16,000mk saved
  • Weekend Rivett work: Use Saturdays/Sundays to buy Rivett and start assembly

Option B: Futufon + Side Hustles

  • Work Futufon Mon-Fri
  • Advertisement delivery on weekends (if profitable)
  • Flea market sales of junk items
  • One firewood run (if 3,200mk confirmed)
  • Potential: 3,500-4,500mk/week
‚úÖ Recommended Timeline

Week 1-2: Futufon only, focus on survival
Week 3-4: Continue Futufon, buy Rivett, start light assembly
Week 5-6: Futufon + order critical parts
Week 7+: Quit Futufon, focus full-time on Rivett build

Late Game (Week 9+): Rivett Build Phase

üîß Strategy: Minimal Work, Maximum Building

At this point: You should have 12,000-20,000mk saved and Rivett partially built

Income strategy shift:

  • Quit Futufon - you have buffer money
  • Focus on Rivett - assembly is time-consuming
  • Emergency income only: If money runs low, do ONE firewood delivery or short-term jobs
  • Part-time advertisement: Can continue if it's profitable

Financial management:

  • Keep 2,000mk emergency fund (survival + fuel)
  • Buy parts in batches (save on parts magazine calls)
  • Prioritize engine/drivetrain parts (get car running first)
  • Cosmetic parts can wait
