What is the VIN System?

Every Corris Rivett in My Winter Car has a unique Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) - a 24-character code that reveals your car's complete factory specification. This system tells you:

üè≠ Factory Details

Manufacturing year (1971-1976), assembly plant location, production month, and serial number

‚öôÔ∏è Mechanical Specs

Engine type, transmission variant, axle ratio, differential type (open or limited-slip)

üé® Appearance

Trim level (L/LX/SLX/GT), original color, vinyl roof options, interior color and features

üí° Why VIN Matters

Understanding your Rivett's VIN is essential for:

  • Authentic restoration - Restore your car to exact factory specification
  • Historical vehicle registration - Required for museum/classic car status (30+ year requirement)
  • Parts sourcing strategy - Know which trim-specific parts to hunt for. See our parts acquisition guide.
  • Collector value - Rare trim levels (especially GT) increase bragging rights
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: VIN is Save-Specific

Your Rivett's VIN resets with each new game save. Every time you start a new game, you'll get a different randomized VIN with different specs. The VIN you decode applies only to that specific save file.

1. Where to Find Your VIN Plate

The VIN plate is located on the front chassis of your Corris Rivett, hidden under where the hood would be.

1 Open the Hood Area

Your Rivett starts as a bare chassis, so the VIN is visible immediately. If you've already installed the hood, you'll need to remove it first.

2 Look Above Right Front Headlight

Exact location: Front of the chassis, directly above the right-side front headlight position.

It appears as a small metal plate/sticker with multiple rows of letters and numbers printed on it.

3 Write Down the Complete Code

The VIN contains 24 characters (combination of letters and numbers). Write it down carefully - you'll need it for decoding tools.

Tip: Take a screenshot or photo of the VIN plate in-game for easy reference.

üö´ Cannot Decode In-Game

My Winter Car does not have built-in VIN decoding. You must use external community-created decoder tools (see section 3 below) or mods to translate the code into readable specifications.

2. Understanding VIN Structure

The 24-character VIN is divided into specific segments, each revealing different information about your Rivett:

UCBBDLC123456 1NAB C
Position 1: Country U = Britain
Position 2: Assembly Plant C = Saarlouis, Germany
Position 3: Model B = Rivett
Position 4: Body Type B = 2-Door Pillared Sedan
Position 5: Trim Level D = L (base) | E = LX | G = SLX | P = GT
Position 6: Year L = 1971 | M = 1972 | N = 1973 | P = 1974 | R = 1975 | S = 1976
Position 7: Month C = Jan | K = Feb | D = Mar ... (12 codes)
Positions 8-13: Serial Number 6-digit unique identifier
Position 14: Drivetrain 1 = RWD (all Rivetts are rear-wheel drive)
Positions 15-16: Engine NA = Standard 2.0L | NE = High Performance 2.0L
Position 17: Transmission B = 4-speed Manual | 7 = 3-speed Automatic
Position 18: Axle Ratio S = 3.44 | B = 3.75 | C = 3.89 | N = 4.11 | E = 4.44

Key VIN Details

Category Possible Values Impact
Manufacturing Year 1971-1976 (randomized) Affects historical registration eligibility (30+ years in 1999 game setting = 1974 or older)
1974 Facelift Code P for 1974+ Visual design changes: updated bumpers, grilles, trim pieces differ from pre-facelift
Assembly Plants Dagenham, Manchester, Saarlouis, Rheine Cosmetic detail only - no gameplay impact
Trim Level L / LX / SLX / GT Major impact - determines brakes, differential, interior, wheels, gauges (see section 4)
Engine Type Standard 2.0L vs High Performance 2.0L HP variant typically comes with sportier trims (SLX/GT)
Transmission 4-speed Manual / 3-speed Auto Automatic transmission exclusive to SLX trim as factory option
Axle Ratio 3.44 to 4.44 range Affects acceleration vs top speed balance. Higher ratio = better acceleration, lower top speed

5. Historical Vehicle Registration

If you restore your Rivett to exact factory VIN specification, you can register it as a historical/museum vehicle with special benefits.

Requirements for Historical Registration

üìã Eligibility Checklist
  1. Age Requirement: Vehicle must be 30+ years old. Since the game is set in 1999, this means your Rivett must be manufactured in January 1974 or earlier (check VIN position 6 for year code L/M/N/early P)
  2. Factory Specification Match: All parts must match the original VIN specification:
    • Correct trim-level parts (L/LX/SLX/GT specific components)
    • Original transmission type (4-speed manual / 3-speed auto)
    • Matching differential and axle ratio
    • Factory-spec interior components (seats, gauges, steering wheel)
    • Original exterior colors and vinyl roof (if applicable)
  3. Complete Restoration: Vehicle must be fully assembled and functional
  4. Inspection Pass: Must pass standard vehicle inspection first (see our troubleshooting guide for common inspection issues)

Benefits of Historical Status

üéØ Longer Inspection Intervals

Historical vehicles are inspected less frequently than regular vehicles. Exact interval difference not yet confirmed in EA, but real-world standards suggest every 2 years vs annually.

üèÜ Collector Achievement

Personal satisfaction of authentic restoration. Demonstrates mastery of the game's parts system and dedication to detail.

üìú Special Documentation

Historical registration paperwork serves as proof of your restoration efforts and VIN-matching achievement.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Clarification

VIN matching is ONLY required for historical registration. You can complete and drive your Rivett with mixed/mismatched parts for regular use. The developer has confirmed: "The VIN on the car does not have to match" for normal gameplay.

Historical registration is an optional challenge for purists and collectors, not mandatory for enjoying the game.

7. Common VIN Questions

Question Answer
Do I NEED to match VIN to complete the Rivett? No. VIN matching is optional. Any parts will work regardless of spec. VIN matching is only required for historical vehicle registration (collector achievement).
Will mismatched parts perform worse? No. A GT steering wheel works identically whether installed on an L or GT trim. Performance is not affected by VIN matching.
Can I change my VIN? No. VIN is locked when you start a new save. Only way to get a different VIN is to start a new game.
What if I want a specific trim (like GT)? Start new saves until you get the trim you want. Check VIN immediately after buying Rivett. GT trim code is P in position 5.
How do I know which parts match my VIN? Use decoder tools to see full spec, then cross-reference with parts lists. Steam community guides show VIN-specific part codes (e.g., VIN412C = GT spoiler bootlid).
Is historical registration worth it? Only if you enjoy collector challenges. The main benefit (longer inspection intervals) is minor. Most players skip VIN matching.
Can I use mods to see VIN in-game? Yes, some mods add in-game VIN display features. Check Nexus Mods or community resources. Our mods guide coming soon.
What's the rarest trim? GT trim is significantly rarer than L/LX/SLX. GT parts also have much lower spawn rates in classifieds (community reports ~5-10% of packages contain GT items).
