üßä Ice Track Race - Complete Guide

What is Jokkis Folk Racing?

Jokkis (Finnish folk racing) is a traditional form of motorsport where modified cars race side-by-side on ice tracks. My Winter Car's version stays true to Finnish tradition with one key difference: you don't have to sell your car after the race (unlike real Jokkis rules).

üéØ Racing Strategies

Ice Track Strategy

Starting Position

  • Qualifying Matters: Saturday qualifying determines Sunday grid. Push hard but don't crash.
  • Clean Start: Ice means low grip. Avoid wheel spin off the line.
  • First Corner Critical: Side-by-side format means aggressive first corner. Be ready for contact.

Ice Driving Techniques

  • Smooth Inputs: Sudden steering/braking = spin. Be gentle and progressive.
  • Brake Early: Ice braking distance is 3-4x normal roads.
  • Momentum: Maintaining speed through corners beats hard acceleration out of them.
  • Line Choice: Rubbered-in racing line may offer more grip than fresh ice.

Overtaking

  • Plan Ahead: Set up overtakes two corners in advance.
  • Slipstream: Use straights to draft and build speed for pass.
  • Defensive Lines: Block inside line on corners to prevent attacks.
  • Avoid Contact: Damage slows you down. Clean racing wins races.

Rally Strategy (General Ice/Snow)

Snow Road Techniques

  • Look Ahead: Snow obscures road surface. Use tree lines to read upcoming corners.
  • Throttle Control: On/off throttle upsets balance. Maintain steady power.
  • Weight Transfer: Use braking to shift weight forward for turn-in grip.
  • Scandinavian Flick: For tight hairpins, use weight transfer to rotate car.

Risk Management

  • Know When to Push: Clean run beats crashed attempt at hero time.
  • Mechanical Sympathy: Breaking car mid-rally = DNF.
  • Weather Adaptation: If snow falls, adjust driving style immediately.

Q: Do I need a separate race car and daily driver?

A: Not necessarily. Until EA finalizes requirements, build a dual-purpose car. If stripping headlights becomes mandatory, reassess.

Q: When does Ice Track race happen?

A: Every other weekend (Friday practice, Saturday qualifying, Sunday finals). Check in-game calendar for exact dates.

Q: What if I crash during the race?

A: Damage mechanics in racing are unconfirmed. Likely: mechanical damage = slower car or DNF. Avoid crashes!

Q: How do I register for races?

A: Registration process awaits EA implementation. Likely: Show up at ice track with legal Rivett during race weekend.

Q: Can I practice on the ice track outside race weekends?

A: Unknown. Check if ice track is accessible during non-race weeks in your save.
