Acquisition Process

üìû How to Purchase
  1. Call 08-609-553 from apartment phone (use numpad)
  2. Let seller finish talking (don't hang up early or call won't register)

  5. Transport shell home using Kekmet tractor

üå®Ô∏è Special Considerations: Environmental and Survival Factors

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Survival Mechanics Affecting Vehicle Choice

Temperature Management

  • Any vehicle without working heater + broken windshield = you get cold and can die
  • Sleeping in unheated vehicle = hypothermia death
  • Only sleep in running, heated vehicles

Snow and Terrain

  • OFF-ROAD RULE: Only Kekmet can drive off-road
  • All other vehicles immediately get stuck in deep snow
  • Even Rivett with performance build gets stuck off paved roads

Cold Starting

  • Block heater is ESSENTIAL for Sorbett
  • Diesel vehicles (Kekmet, Gifu) have cold start challenges
  • Battery drain is real - keep vehicles plugged in when possible

‚ùå Common Vehicle Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Mistake #1: Forgetting Block Heater

Problem: Sorbett won't start, or oil damage accumulates

Solution: ALWAYS plug in when parked at home, ALWAYS unplug before driving

Mistake #2: Taking Wrong Vehicle Off-Road

Problem: Sorbett/Rivett stuck in snow, potentially hours from help

Solution: Use only Kekmet for any off-road or deep snow driving

Mistake #3: Rushing Rivett Build

Problem: Missing parts, incorrect assembly, permanent engine damage

Solution: Take time, read manuals, source quality parts, test thoroughly

Mistake #4: Over-Revving Rivett

Problem: No rev limiter = catastrophic engine failure

Solution: Install tachometer immediately, watch RPMs religiously, avoid money shifts

Mistake #5: Using Kekmet for Daily Errands

Problem: Extremely slow speed wastes real-world time

Solution: Use Sorbett for anything on paved roads in town

Mistake #6: Ignoring Vehicle Temperature Systems

Problem: Death from hypothermia, or overheating engines

Solution: Monitor heaters, let engines warm up, watch temperature gauges

‚ùì FAQ: Vehicle Comparison Questions

Q: Which vehicle should I focus on first?
A: Master the Sorbett completely before attempting Rivett build. Sorbett is free, reliable, and teaches you winter driving mechanics.
Q: Can I sell the Sorbett to fund my Rivett?
A: Not currently possible in EA. Sorbett is your permanent backup vehicle.
Q: Do I need the Kekmet if I have a Sorbett?
A: YES. Kekmet is REQUIRED to transport your Rivett shell after purchase, and it's the only vehicle for off-road access.
Q: Which transmission should I choose for the Rivett?
A: Depends on VIN and preference. 4-speed is stock/reliable, 5-speed better for highway, auto easier but less engaging.
Q: Is the Gifu required for progression?
A: No, septic jobs are optional. But it's a good income source if you need money.
Q: Can I drive multiple vehicles at once?
A: No, but you can park them strategically around the map for different purposes.
Q: What if my Sorbett breaks down?
A: In current EA, Sorbett is relatively reliable and has limited maintenance. Major failure unlikely but keep it plugged in.
