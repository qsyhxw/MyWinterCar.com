JokkeNeighbor

Apartment Neighbor & Kilju Buyer

Jokke is your neighbor in the adjacent apartment. He's one of two drunk neighbors who provide a valuable survival resource: free alcohol to manage your Problem Bar when you're broke.

üìç Location & Access

Location
Adjacent apartment to yours
Access Method
Hop fence to backyard
Best Time
Night (when not working)

üçª Free Alcohol Source

When you're broke and your Problem Bar is rising, Jokke offers a critical lifeline:

  • Access: Visit neighbors at night by hopping the fence
  • Cost: Completely free (no payment required)
  • Effect: Lowers Problem Bar same as purchased beer
  • Frequency: Can visit repeatedly as needed
üíÄ CRITICAL WARNING: Neighbor's Vodka

DO NOT drink vodka from their kitchen counter. Multiple players report drinking neighbor's vodka caused 6-7 day blackouts, effectively ending their playthrough.

Safe Strategy: Stick to beer only. Avoid strong spirits.

üí∞ Kilju Sales

Jokke buys homemade kilju (sugar wine), providing income opportunity:

  • Transaction: Sell kilju for cash
  • Verification: Jokke makes you drink from what you're selling (quality check)
  • Access: Must catch him when he's home (not passed out or working)
‚ö†Ô∏è Spawn Behavior Notes
  • Empty Apartment Issue: Some players report Jokke's apartment appears empty
  • Possible Fix: May be save-dependent or require specific triggers
  • MSC Editor Workaround: Advanced users report changing "drunkstage" value to 0
  • Best Practice: If apartment empty, try visiting different times/days

üí° Survival Strategy

Early Game Lifesaver: When you've spent all money on Rivett parts and rent, Jokke's free alcohol prevents Problem Bar death. This is especially critical before your first job paycheck arrives.

Related Info: Learn more about managing the Problem Bar in our Troubleshooting Guide.

üìû How to Buy the Rivett

‚úÖ Complete Purchase Process
  1. Find Advertisement: Look for Rivett sale poster inside PSK or at gas station
  2. Call Using Numpad: Dial 08609553 from apartment or parents' house phone
  3. CRITICAL: Wait for entire conversation ‚Äî DO NOT hang up early!
  4. Check Map: "Car for sale" marker should appear (if not, call failed)
  5. Visit Daytime: Drive to location during daylight hours (he won't appear at night)
  6. Complete Transaction: Approach Reijo, wait for dialogue, pay 500mk
  7. Receive Car: Rivett shell + 10-12 random parts on ground nearby
‚ùå Common Purchase Failures
  • Hanging Up Early: #1 mistake ‚Äî map marker won't appear if you hang up before seller finishes
  • Night Visit: Reijo only spawns during daytime, arriving at night wastes trip
  • Wrong Number Format: Don't include dash ‚Äî type 08609553 not "08-609 553"
  • Using Number Row: Must use keyboard NUMPAD, not top row numbers

üöó What You Get

  • Rivett Shell: Empty body/frame (no engine, transmission, or interior)
  • 10-12 Random Parts: Scattered on ground near car
  • Towing Required: Cannot drive ‚Äî use Kekmet tractor or Gifu to tow
  • Destination Choice: Many players tow to parents' house for garage access
üí° Post-Purchase Notes

After buying Rivett, you'll need ~8,000-14,000mk more for parts, plus weekly rent (525mk) and bills. Start a job immediately to fund the build.

From My Summer Car: Reijo's character returns from MSC where he bought firewood. In MWC, his role simplified to single Rivett transaction.

üíî What Happened to Uncle

Between My Summer Car (1995) and My Winter Car (1999), Uncle Toivo's health catastrophically declined:

‚ö†Ô∏è Tragic Transformation
  • Cancer Diagnosis: Likely lung cancer from excessive smoking
  • Extreme Weight Loss: Now looks skeletal (lost massive amount of weight)

üöõ The Gifu Connection

In My Summer Car: Uncle owned and drove the Gifu 405 T septic truck, which he gave to the player after losing his driver's license (DUI/speeding).

In My Winter Car: The player already owns the Gifu from the start (inheritance from MSC story). Toivo no longer drives and is essentially waiting to die.

üìù Cautionary Tale

The Fridge Note: At your parents' house, there's a note warning: "Stop drinking alcohol or you'll end up like uncle."

This directly references Toivo's decline. Your character starts as an alcoholic (empty beer bottles in apartment), and the game warns you about following uncle's path.

üí° Current Role in MWC

  • Narrative Purpose: Serves as cautionary tale about alcoholism and smoking
  • Emotional Impact: MSC veterans will feel impact of seeing beloved character dying
  • Visit Option: Can visit him in retirement home for somber moment

Related Info: You inherit Uncle's Gifu truck and can use it for septic pumping jobs. Learn about managing the Problem Bar to avoid Uncle's fate.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why can't I find Jokke in his apartment?

A: Some players report Jokke's apartment appears empty. This may be save-dependent or require specific triggers. Try visiting different times/days. Advanced solution: MSC Editor can change "drunkstage" value to 0 in save file.

Q: Can I still get the Gifu from Uncle Toivo?

A: No. In MWC, you already own the Gifu from the start (story continuation from MSC). Uncle Toivo is bedridden in retirement home and no longer functional for gameplay.

Q: Where did Teimo's shop go?

A: PSK's modern facilities drove Teimo's shop out of business between 1995-1999. He now only runs Pub Nappo. All shopping functions moved to PSK.

Q: How do I know when Fleetari's shop is open?

A: Monday-Friday 8:00-16:00 only. Door will be locked outside these hours. Weekends completely closed.

Q: Can I permanently fail the taxi job?

A: Yes! If you miss the 12:00 PM meeting at PSK after calling, don't enter passenger seat, or interrupt dialogue, you're locked out permanently with no rehire chance. Jail time also voids opportunity.

Q: Is Uncle Toivo going to die during gameplay?

A: Unknown in current EA version. Wiki states "death is most likely right around the corner" but actual death event not confirmed. He may remain as static bedridden NPC.

Q: Why won't Reijo appear when I visit to buy the Rivett?

A: Three common causes: (1) Visiting at night (he only appears daytime), (2) Hung up phone call early (map marker didn't trigger), (3) Used wrong phone number format.
