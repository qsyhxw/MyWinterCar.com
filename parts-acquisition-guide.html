üéØ Parts System Overview

The Corris Rivett requires 100+ parts to assemble from an empty shell into a driveable state (confirmed by developer Toplessgun). For complete assembly walkthrough, see our Rivett Build Guide.

Unlike My Summer Car, parts are not scattered around the map but must be purchased through three main channels:

üìç Fleetari Repair Shop
New Parts | High Price
üìç AMIS Catalog
Basic Parts | Mid Price
üìç Classifieds Magazine
Used Parts | Low Price
‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Restriction - Unique Parts

The following parts can only be purchased ONCE (developer confirmed):

  • Engine Block - Cannot build spare engines
  • Cylinder Head - If damaged, must repair or replace (expensive)
  • Steering Column - Unique restriction

üí° All other parts can be purchased multiple times as spares (limited only by PC performance)

üí° Set Purchase Mechanic

Certain parts are purchased as sets rather than individually (developer confirmed Jun 2023):

  • Bearings - Purchase complete set at once
  • Pistons - Purchase complete set at once
  • Rockers - Purchase complete set at once

This aligns with realistic repair practices and simplifies the purchasing process.

üì¶ Packaging Method

Parts come in individual boxes, which are then placed into a shopping bag (like Teimo's grocery store). You cannot carry unlimited items, so plan your shopping trips accordingly.

‚ö†Ô∏è Stock Limitation Critical Notice
  • Limited Stock: Fleetari has finite quantities - when sold out, they're gone
  • Random Restock: Developer hasn't decided restock intervals yet (as of May 2024)
  • Strategy Tip: Buy critical parts immediately when you see them
  • EA Status: "I haven't decided the full inventory of Fleetari's" - Toplessgun

Purchase Workflow

  1. Drive to Fleetari Repair Shop in Loppe
  2. Note business hours: Monday-Friday 08:00-16:00
  3. Enter shop, parts shelf is near the front door
  4. Check available parts (displayed on shelf)
  5. Click parts to add to shopping bag
  6. Go to counter to pay
  7. Carry shopping bag home (mind carry limits)
üí∞ Pricing Strategy

Fleetari's parts are brand new, so they're the most expensive. If budget is tight, prioritize buying from Fleetari:

  • Unique parts (Engine block, Cylinder head, Steering column)
  • Critical engine internals (for performance assurance)
  • Safety-related parts (brakes, steering)

Non-critical parts can be purchased used from Classifieds to save budget.

Fleetari Repair Services (Additional Functions)

Fleetari doesn't just sell parts - he also provides professional repair services:

  • Payment Timing: Pay after work is done (unlike MSC which requires prepayment)
  • Key Management: Fleetari takes your keys during repairs, returns them after payment
  • Completion Time: Randomized based on work complexity
    • Tire job / Basic maintenance - Fast
    • Painting / Bodywork - Slow
  • Tire Service: Choose to change 1-4 tires, price adjusts by quantity
üöó Bachglotz Loaner Service

When you leave your Rivett at Fleetari for repairs, you can borrow Fleetari's Bachglotz (1965 Oldsmobile 98) so you don't have to walk home.

Use Cases

The AMIS Catalog provides several basic and essential parts, suitable for quickly obtaining core components needed to start the vehicle in early game. New players should check our Beginner's Guide for first-time AMIS catalog usage.

‚ö†Ô∏è EA Version Limitation

Developer confirmed (Dec 2025): AMIS Parts catalog currently missing interior tuning stuff. These items are planned for future EA updates.

If you can't find a part in AMIS, try Fleetari or Classifieds.

Usage Workflow

  1. Go to parents' house garage (next to woodshed)
  2. Find AMIS catalog (usually on or near workbench)
  3. Open catalog, flip through pages to see available parts
  4. Click part prices to select
  5. Confirm order (similar to Classifieds process)
  6. Wait for delivery (delivery time varies)
  7. Check mailbox for pickup notice
  8. Go to PSK to pay and collect
üí° AMIS vs Classifieds Difference
  • AMIS: Fixed catalog, limited part types but stable and reliable
  • Classifieds: Weekly refresh, many part types but randomized

Tip: Check AMIS first (quick), if part not available, wait for Classifieds refresh.

üé≤ Randomness Mechanic

Classifieds parts are randomly generated. Some non-essential wearable parts are only available from flea market (developer confirmed). Players report spending long periods waiting for specific parts to appear (e.g., front left brake assembly).

Strategy: Check Classifieds weekly, order needed parts immediately when you see them, even if not urgently needed.

‚ö†Ô∏è Complete Ordering Process (Critical Steps)

Step-by-step (each step matters - miss one and order fails):

  1. Buy Magazine:
    • Drive to PSK Gas Station
    • Purchase Classifieds Magazine (usually at counter or magazine rack)
  2. Find Phone:
    • Can use apartment phone or any public phone
    • Pick up receiver
  3. Open Magazine:
    • Press F to open Classifieds Magazine
    • Find the part ad you want
    • Note the phone number on ad
  4. Dial Number (CRITICAL):
    • MUST use NUMPAD, cannot use number row!
    • Enter complete phone number
    • Press Enter to call
  5. Wait for Them to Hang Up (CRITICAL):
    • NEVER hang up early
    • Patiently wait for seller to finish talking
    • Wait for them to hang up (line goes dead)
    • If number below ad turns to 0, order successful
    • If not 0, you made a mistake - retry
  6. Wait for Delivery:
    • Delivery time: ~40-200 in-game minutes (player tested)
    • Maximum wait could be 12000 seconds (200 minutes)
    • Can do other things during this time
  7. Check Mailbox:
    • After delivery, you'll receive pickup notice in apartment mailbox
    • Open mailbox, take out notice
  8. Go to PSK to Collect:
    • Bring pickup notice, drive to PSK
    • Pay at counter (wait for clerk to stand at counter)
    • Receive package after payment
‚ùå Common Mistakes (Cause Order Failure)
  1. Using number row instead of NUMPAD - Dial doesn't work
  2. Hanging up phone early - Order not submitted (ad number won't turn to 0)
  3. Forgetting to check mailbox - Part delivered but you don't know
  4. Trying to pay before clerk is at counter - Cannot interact

üì¶ Set Purchase Parts

These parts must be bought as complete sets (developer confirmed Jun 2023):

  • Bearings - Includes all main bearings and rod bearings
  • Pistons - All 4 pistons purchased at once
  • Rockers - Complete rocker arm set
üí° Why Set Purchase?

Developer explained: "it makes more sense" (more realistic). In actual car repair, these parts are indeed replaced as sets to ensure even wear and consistent performance.

üîß Wear & Failure Mechanics

Approximately 50-60% of parts can break (developer estimated Dec 2023):

  • Visual symptoms: Rotating parts show scuff marks (wear marks)
  • Functional symptoms: Performance degradation, unusual noises
  • Sound symptoms: Abnormal sounds
üîç Diagnosing Wear

Developer's specially designed visual feedback:

  • Bearing surfaces show visible scuff marks when getting bad
  • Rotating part surfaces display wear marks to help check wear level
  • Some parts require starting engine or driving to discover issues

üÜò Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why doesn't Fleetari have a certain part?

A: Fleetari's stock is limited and restocks randomly. Developer stated "I haven't decided the full inventory yet" (as of May 2024). If out of stock, try Classifieds or wait for restock.

Q: I'm overwhelmed - where do I even start buying parts?

A: Follow our Corris Rivett Build Guide which provides a step-by-step parts acquisition checklist with priorities. Start with: (1) Engine block + Cylinder head from Fleetari (unique parts), (2) Basic essentials from AMIS, (3) Body parts from Classifieds.

Q: How long after ordering from Classifieds does delivery take?

A: Based on player testing (MSC Editor verified), delivery time is approximately 40-200 in-game minutes. Maximum wait could be 12000 seconds (200 minutes). Check apartment mailbox for pickup notice.

Q: I've waited 1 hour, parts still haven't arrived?

A: Confirm: (1) Did ad number turn to 0 before hanging up? (2) Did you use NUMPAD to dial? (3) Did you wait for them to hang up? If all correct, you may have unlucky 200-minute max wait. Keep waiting and checking mailbox.

Q: Where is the AMIS catalog?

A: In parents' house garage (Parents' Garage), usually on or near the workbench. Same location as My Summer Car.

Q: Are used parts worth buying?

A: For non-critical parts (body panels, interior trim, etc.) absolutely. But for engine internals and safety parts (brakes), recommend buying new for performance assurance.

Q: What if engine block is damaged?

A: Engine block is a unique part - can only repair, not replace. Send to Fleetari for repair (expensive). Prevention: monitor temperature, avoid over-revving (no rev limiter!).

Q: How to know if parts are damaged?

A: Developer designed multi-symptom system: (1) Visual - bearing surfaces have scuff marks; (2) Functional - performance degradation; (3) Sound - abnormal noises. Some parts require starting engine or driving to discover.

Q: Can I buy all parts from Fleetari?

A: No. Fleetari primarily sells engine parts and wear items. Some parts (especially body parts and interior) need to be obtained from Classifieds or AMIS.
