1. What is a Facelift?

In automotive terminology, a "facelift" (also called a "refresh" or "mid-cycle update") refers to design changes made to a car model partway through its production run. Rather than a completely new generation, a facelift updates the visual styling and sometimes adds new features while keeping the core platform unchanged.

üöó Why Manufacturers Do Facelifts
  • Keep the design fresh and competitive in the market
  • Address early production issues
  • Incorporate new safety or comfort features
  • Reduce costs with updated manufacturing processes

In My Winter Car, the Corris Rivett comes in both pre-facelift and facelift versions, determined by its in-game manufacturing year. This affects the visual appearance of your build!

4. Grille Differences

The front grille is one of the most visually distinctive differences between the two generations:

Pre-Facelift Grille (1971-1973)

  • Material: Chrome/chrome-black options
  • Design: Traditional upright style
  • Badge: Corris badge present
  • Trim Options: Multiple grille options by trim
  • Feel: Classic, premium chrome look

Facelift Grille (1974-1976)

  • Material: Single-piece plastic
  • Design: Forward-sloping, aggressive
  • Badge: No Corris badge (only grill without it)
  • Color: Body color, paintable
  • Feel: Modern, sporty appearance
üé® Facelift Grille is Paintable

The facelift plastic grille can be painted to match your body color, allowing for a cohesive, integrated front-end look. This is a unique customization option not available on pre-facelift models.

5. Bumper Differences

The bumper design evolved significantly between generations:

Pre-Facelift Bumpers

  • Material: Full chrome steel
  • Shape: Smooth, rounded edges
  • Style: Classic 1970s chrome era
  • Finish: Polished chrome

Facelift Bumpers

  • Material: Chrome with plastic elements
  • Shape: More square, angular
  • Style: Transitioning to modern plastic
  • Feature: Includes plastic strip insert
‚ö†Ô∏è Parts Compatibility

Pre-facelift and facelift bumpers are NOT interchangeable. Make sure to acquire the correct bumper for your model year when sourcing parts.

8. Real-World History

The Corris Rivett is based on the Ford Taunus TC1. The real-world facelift occurred in September 1973:

Aspect Details
Real-World Model Ford Taunus TC1 (1970-1976)
Facelift Date September 1973 (for 1974 model year)
Key Changes Rectangular headlamps (previously only on XL+), new grilles, revised dashboard
Interior More angular, ergonomically improved dashboard
Chassis Added front and rear stabilizer bars
üìú Historical Context

The early 1970s saw a gradual shift in automotive design from heavy chrome to plastic exterior elements. This was driven by cost reduction, weight savings, and changing aesthetic preferences. The Ford Taunus TC1 facelift reflects this transition perfectly ‚Äî a trend that My Winter Car accurately recreates.
